The emergence of the Internet led to a period, also brought a lot of convenience to people's life, and the wave of Internet online shopping has gradually become another mainstream network service. Students are highly active group in second-hand items market because of their frequent relocations and same demand of saving money. However, the research results showed that school students now do not have a very good handling for second-hand resources, which not only wastes the resources, also can't save economic cost. Based on these characteristics,This project studies and designs a campus second-hand market App, expounds the implement and main functions of campus second-hand market application system. The project designed and implemented commodity information browsing, commodity classification inquiries, online communication, bidding and profile management system, and other functions. It provides a quick and convenient online trading platform mainly for students’ second-hand goods, makes students in need access to cheap and satisfied second-hand items and let them get . It is both environmental and economic, and has high practical value.

Background

In school, especially many second-hand goods are thrown away in universities and colleges, which both damages the environment, and wastes of resources. For such rich second-hand resources, there should be an reasonable way to handle it, an Internet platform is in need to make full use of the mature technology, let the students used resources can be reasonable shared and effectively used so as to make resources communication on campus, save cost, and make waste useful. So create school second-hand trading platform is feasible and has certain practical value. IdleGo is a mobile app designed on iOS that aims to facilitate used item exchange within college students. It took the pros and cons of the existing second-hand Apps into consideration and designed specifically for college students with enhanced safety measures and fine-tuned for their needs. IdleGo is aimed to provide a perfect platform for students safely and efficiently exchange their items and also ensure sellers and buyers meet their expectation for prices of goods.

Research

Research Goals

1. Find out how students used different second-hand platforms
2. Investigate the common characteritics of the second-hand exchange experience by students
3. Determine the most needed and effective ways to help students get their items in need

Research Method

1. Online survey
2. Interviews

Major Findings

77% of survey participants use facebook platform to buy/sell their second hand product.
46% of survey participants said that it took less than an hour to find what they want but it always took more than a day to a week to get/sell the object.
92% said that they concerns the condition of the items, then the price and whether the seller/buyer is trustworthy.
The most popular items that have been exchanged among students were books, electronics, stationary, personal items.

Problem Statement

“Campus students need a quick and convenient online trading platform to help them exchange their idle goods for the sake of saving money and build an environmental friendly society”

Feasibility Analysis

1. Technical feasibility
Our developers and designers are college students who have laptops or desktop computers. From the aspects of software technology, we decided to use react native which is a programming language has been very mature, and the system function completely can be achieved.
2. Operation feasibility
The application interface design fully takes into account the habits of stakeholders. The interface has to be user-friendly and attractive, and also very simple for operation.
3. Economic feasibility
The cost of this system is mainly from the cost of software development, and system only requires students to have mobile phones. The object of the application is college students. Students are a group of people with relatively small income, therefore, second-hand goods have more markets for college students. This application can not only save resources, but also save money for low-income college students. So, it is feasible in economy.
