A little bit more about me...

Hey, It's Daisy

I'm currently a graduate student from Cornell University@information science. Good design, in my opinion, is not about flashy interfaces, but rather about what goes on behind the scenes to assist people achieve their goals. Delivering solutions with maximum utility, logic, and visual appeal fascinates me.

With double majors in Human Biology and Cognitive Science specialized in Human-Computer Interaction from UC San Diego, I view the mind as a scientist and a storyteller. I understand both the biological basis of thought and the contextual/inner factors that impact how human perceive outside world, which gives me solid knowledge to support each of my design decisions. My experience being a foodie blogger taught me about subtle visual change and information hierarchy impact audience’s preference. My volunteer experience in hospital give important of inclusive design

I utilize my diverse expertise to develop useable items that can be incorporated into the complexity of human behavior, combining knowledge and enthusiasm from different fields to improve user experience. I'm particularly fascinated by the perplexing design difficulties in the healthcare area.
